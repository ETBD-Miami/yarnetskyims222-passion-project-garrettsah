Activities

Cleveland is a melting pot of various cultures and backgrounds, having been settled around the age of the Industrial Revolution. During this time, Cleveland became a hub for coal, iron ore and steel production. By 1880, 28% of the city's jobs were based around the steel industry. Although Cleveland has struggled economically throughout the years, the city is still known as a place where cultural and social activities are strong. The city's first amusement park opened in 1894, which was officially named Euclid Beach Park but became affectionately known as Coney Island. Cleveland also has an extremely rich history in music, being the hometown of many great artists, such as Art Tatum, Bone Thugs-N-Harmony, Bootsy Collins, and Kid Cudi (songs accessible below).
